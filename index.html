RETI Center Community Solar Outreach Tool

This interactive map illustrates the suitability of buildings across NYC for hosting a rooftop community solar panel array. I conducted a thorough analysis of NYC buildings for RETI Center to support its Local Power program in expanding to more sites across the city. This tool identifies clusters of highly suitable buildings (called campaign zones) and can be used as a strategy tool for outreach to property owners at suitable sites.

Midtown Bus Terminal Capstone Project

This analysis of temporary bus operations at the Port Authority's Midtown Bus Terminal was my capstone group project as part of my NYU Masters of Urban Planning. Given the limited capacity of the bus terminal during its upcoming reconstruction, our team identified alternative terminals to accommodate rush hour buses and analyzed bus routes to determine which could be rerouted to each terminal with minimal impacts on travelers.

Solar Power and MTA Zero Emission Buses

Add two-page side-by-side of the website here?

This report presents some of the opportunities that the MTA could harness during its transition to an all-electric bus fleet. Working as a Senior Graduate Researcher at the NYU Rudin Center, I analyzed the MTA's published zero-emission transition plan and conducted a geospatial analysis of the agency's existing bus depots to identify how much potential electricity could be generated by rooftop solar at the depots. I also discuss some of the challenges the agency faces in trying something so bold and ambitious, and suggest some means of overcoming those challenges.

NYC Green Infrastructure Analysis Article

This article told the story of the rollout of green infrastructure across NYC. I analyzed NYC DEP Open Data on the location and types of green infrastructure that has been built across the city and identified some interesting trends in the types of infrastructure installed in certain neighborhoods. The research was ultimately published in the Wagner Planner, the independent student journal of the Urban Planning Student Association at NYU Wagner.

WorkAdvance 7-Year Impact Report

I was the lead author of this MDRC report analyzing the long-term impacts of an innovative sectoral workforce development program, WorkAdvance. The report tracked the long-term employment and earnings of participants in the program at four organizations across the country and compared them to a randomized control sample. For this report, I added an racial equity analysis that explored the economic impacts of this program by the race and ethnicity of participants.
